Kullu

Himachal Pradesh

1. The "Valley of Gods" Kullu is basically a slice of heaven in Himachal Pradesh. It’s famously called the "Valley of Gods" or Dev Bhoomi because people believe it was once home to many deities. Every little village there has its own god that locals worship with so much love. It’s located right on the banks of the Beas River, making it look like a postcard! 2. Super Old History Kullu isn’t just some new hill station; it’s actually one of the oldest states in India. Back in the day, it was called Kulanthpitha, which literally translates to "the end of the habitable world." Imagine being so far up in the mountains that people thought nothing existed beyond it! It’s even mentioned in ancient books like the Ramayana and Mahabharata. 3. The Epic Kullu Dussehra If you visit in October, you’ll see the world-famous Kullu Dussehra. Unlike other places where they burn effigies, here they have a huge week-long fair at Dhalpur Maidan. Hundreds of local gods are brought down from the mountains in colorful palanquins to pay respect to Lord Raghunath. It’s a total vibe with music, dancing, and shopping! 4. Adventure Central For anyone who loves a bit of a thrill, Kullu is the place to be. You can go river rafting in the icy waters of the Beas, or try paragliding to see the whole valley from the sky like a bird. There’s also zorbing and trekking. It’s definitely not a place where you’ll ever get bored! 5. Those Famous Shawls & Caps Kullu is super famous for its hand-loomed woolens. You’ve probably seen the Kullu shawls and caps with those bright, colorful geometric patterns. They are super warm and look really classy. Buying one from the local markets is a must-do for anyone visiting. 6. Nature at its Best The valley is packed with thick forests of Pine and Deodar trees. Everywhere you look, there are lush green hills and snowy peaks in the distance. Plus, Kullu is the "Apple Valley" of India! You’ll find huge apple orchards everywhere, and the fruit is so juicy and sweet compared to what we get in the cities. 7. Great Himalayan National Park Just nearby is the GHNP, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It’s a massive park where you can find rare animals like the Snow Leopard and the Blue Sheep. It’s a dream spot for trekkers and people who love wildlife photography. 8. The Mystical Bijli Mahadev One of the coolest spots is the Bijli Mahadev Temple. It’s on a high hill, and legend says that every year, a massive bolt of lightning strikes the Shiva Lingam inside and breaks it into pieces. The priest then puts it back together using butter! The trek up there is a bit tiring but the view is totally worth it. 9. Religious Vibes at Manikaran A short drive from Kullu takes you to Manikaran, which is famous for its hot sulfur springs. People believe the water has healing powers. There’s a beautiful Gurudwara and a Temple right next to each other. It’s so cool because the water is so hot they actually cook rice and langar in it! 10. A Perfect Escape Whether it's the chilly winters with a bit of snow or the pleasant summers, Kullu is the perfect getaway. It’s a mix of peaceful spirituality and high-energy adventure. Honestly, once you visit, you’ll just want to stay there forever and soak in the mountain air.
